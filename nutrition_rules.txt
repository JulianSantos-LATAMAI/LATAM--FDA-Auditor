[SOURCE: FDA 21 CFR 101.9 - Nutrition Labeling]

[RULE: MANDATORY_NUTRIENTS_ORDER]
The Nutrition Facts panel must list nutrients in the following vertical sequence.
NOTE: Standard visual separators (hairlines, white space, or small gaps) between nutrients are PERMITTED for readability, provided the sequence is correct.

1. Calories
2. Total Fat
3. Saturated Fat
4. Trans Fat
5. Cholesterol
6. Sodium
7. Total Carbohydrate
8. Dietary Fiber
9. Total Sugars
10. Includes Xg Added Sugars (Must be indented below Total Sugars)
11. Protein
12. Vitamin D
13. Calcium
14. Iron
15. Potassium

[RULE: FONT_SIZES]
- The title "Nutrition Facts" must be the largest type on the label (larger than 16 point).
- The numerical value for "Calories" must be at least 22 point font size.
- The headings "Serving size" and "Servings per container" must be at least 10 point bold.
- Nutrient names (e.g., "Total Fat", "Sodium") must be at least 8 point font.

[RULE: VISUAL_FORMAT]
- "Calories" must be in bold type.
- "Added Sugars" must be declared as "Includes X g Added Sugars".
- A thick bold line must separate "Calories" from the nutrient list.
- Vitamins and minerals must declare both the quantitative amount (e.g., "20 mg") and the Percent Daily Value (% DV).

[RULE: ROUNDING]
- If Calories are less than 50, round to nearest 5 increments.
- If Calories are greater than 50, round to nearest 10 increments.
- Total Fat: If less than 0.5g, express as 0g.
